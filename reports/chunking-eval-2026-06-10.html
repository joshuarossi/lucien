The local model reads topic structure like an expert but fumbles the mechanics. In 14 of 26 conversations its segmentation was essentially identical to the ideal; in the other 12 the disagreements were structural quibbles, not failures of comprehension. Zero major disagreements. Its labels are consistently specific and accurate (mean 91). The real defects are clerical: 3 chunks anchor to message UUIDs that don't exist (twice it pasted the conversation UUID as a start anchor, once it spliced two UUIDs together), and it occasionally orphans the final substantive message of a thread. Those are exactly the failure modes a small local model shows on long-ID transcription — and exactly the kind a validation pass can catch.

§03Recommendations

Validate anchors at insert time

All 3 hallucinated UUIDs were near-misses (conversation UUID pasted as start anchor ×2; two message UUIDs spliced together ×1). In chunk-recent.ts, before insertChunk.run(...), verify both UUIDs exist in the loaded message list — and when one doesn't, snap to the nearest plausible message (first message for a bad start, the message whose UUID shares the longest prefix for a splice) or re-prompt for that one chunk. This single check converts every hard failure in this run into a non-event.

Check tail coverage

The model dropped the final substantive exchange three times (trade report msg 34, MLX msgs 52–53, plus farewell tails). After parsing, diff the union of chunk ranges against the message list; if a trailing substantive message is uncovered, extend the last chunk. Cheap, deterministic, and recovers the most-recent — often most-valuable — content.

Tame the overlap instinct

The policy allows single-message dual-membership overlap, but the model twice produced multi-message envelopes (a 6-message overlap in the Ferrari conversation; a chunk fully containing another in the eval conversation). Consider tightening Meta/Chunking.md: "chunks may share at most one boundary message" — the model follows the Meta docs, so this is a config edit, not a code change.

Accept the granularity as-is

The model leans finer-grained than the ideal (29 chunks vs ~20 on the MLX conversation), but per Meta/Chunking.md aspect sub-chunks are explicitly permitted, and thin buckets are fine downstream. No action needed unless cluster noise becomes visible at synthesis.
